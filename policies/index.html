For the Alliance.

Here's a quick rundown of what we will do if we get elected:

  • Extensive Superior Court reforms for transparency and accessibility
    The Superior Court of the Cupertino Alliance has suffered from a severe lack of development and activity since it was created, only being used intermittently for handling complaints and suspending delegates. The Cupertino Alliance Superior Court has not seen a court case in its history. Even if a case were to come up in the Superior Court, there is no place to hold said case. In addition, we have discovered that the methods for filing a case in the Superior Court are far from optimal.

  • Writing a new Charter to futureproof the Cupertino Alliance, to be completed by December 2021
    A work of art when it was first implemented, the Charter of the Cupertino Alliance Act, 2020 has become outdated for the modern day. If elected, a Hamilton-Bornstein administration will continue the work of the Ross-era Charter Committee. We pledge to have written a new Charter for the Cupertino Alliance by the end of December 2021. This new Charter will bring in all of the reforms of our administration into one new document, which will serve as an effective Charter for the Alliance for several terms to come.

  • Membership reforms including a new application form, better remarks and better utilisation of the CASC
    We've spoken to several individuals, many of whom were once potential Cupertino Alliance members, who decided not to apply to join our organisation. Asking them why they decided not to join, it is clear that several issues have arisen in the application process. The only ways to apply to join the Cupertino Alliance is to go onto MicroWiki, onto the Cupertino Alliance page, and click the application form link on the page, or to ask another delegate for the application link. We will make sure that the application is posted prominently on the Cupertino Alliance server. We see membership as an ongoing topic in the Cupertino Alliance, which is why we will shift our priorities for membership to the ongoing development of members as they grow in the Alliance. Due to this change in direction for the organisation, we would like to rename the Ministry of Membership Attainment to the Ministry of Membership and Recruitment, to better reflect the ongoing commitment to this organisation's members. The rest of our concerns for the membership process in this organisation have been answered below with several new solutions, to revitalise the Ministry of Membership Attainment.

  • Mutual cooperation with GUM and regional organisations for events, diplomacy and recruitment
    The Hamilton-Bornstein campaign is committed to establishing several outreach programs with other micronational organisations, in order to strengthen diplomatic ties between the Cupertino Alliance and other organisations. We would also look to host joint events with these organisations, such as a virtual summit to raise funds for charity, as mentioned previously in this manifesto. These outreach programs will help recruit members from these organisations into the Cupertino Alliance, and vice versa via recruiting drives.

  • Ministry changes including the renaming of some ministries, and the creation of a Ministry of Statistical Information
    One of the major policies of the Hamilton-Bornstein campaign is reforming the cabinet of the Cupertino Alliance for the future. The greatest issue currently facing our cabinet is inactivity. Several ministries have either been made redundant by other ministries or have no work to do. As a result of this, we have decided that, if elected, we will make major changes to the ministries of the Alliance, by merging several ministries together, forming new ones, and also renaming some ministries to better reflect their ongoing purpose. We will also be using a monthly task list system, which is essentially a list of jobs that each ministry must complete each month. We predict that using this system, productivity within the Cupertino Alliance cabinet will exponentially increase. We also plan to make things more democratic within our Alliance. Instead of creating a predetermined list of cabinet picks, we will publicly open nominations for all ministerial positions. This will allow for a more diverse and democratic cabinet, instead of one restricted to a certain group of people.

  • Creation and revival of several projects and events such as a 24-hour session for charity, Cupertino Guides and CAoNI as well as other new projects
    Projects are an essential part of the Cupertino Alliance. As important as they are, unfortunately many with great potential have been left to gather dust. We are dedicated to bringing these projects back, as well as taking our organisation into the future with brand new ones.

  • Maintaining our social media presence with a consistently active Twitter and Instagram account, and updating CA MicroWiki pages
    In order to continue to grow in size as an organisation, we need to increase our public visibility. The Hamilton-Bornstein campaign has devised a plan to bring different, often forgotten about sectors of the micronational community together by advertising the Cupertino Alliance on places like Twitter and Instagram. A bit closer to home, we would also like to maintain an active presence on MicroWiki, to show to the well-renowned MicroWiki community that we are a high-quality organisation.
